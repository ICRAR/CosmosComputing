Cosmos Computing

About

Cosmos Computing is a teaching initiative developed by the Outreach and Education department at the International Centre for Radio Astronomy Research (ICRAR), Perth, Western Australia. The purpose of the activity is to give students a sense of how computers work by having them act out a simulation of how the computer 'thinks', and draw conclusions about the nature of computing and programming. The end result of the simulation is that students will have drawn a picture on a grid (like the one above), which acts as a computer display for the purpose of the exercise. This website helps teachers and outreach professionals easily generate a list of instructions that describe how to draw a picture of their design.

How to Use

Clicking on a square on the grid above will fill it in with a chosen colour (default is Black). To remove a square in the case of a miss-click or the design changes, simply click on it again and it will be unfilled. To change the colour of the squares, simply click on a colour button, and click onto the square you want filled. When you have finish your picture, click on the 'Generate Instructions' button below the colour buttons. This will autogenerate AND auto-highlight a set of instructions - all you need to do is Control- or Command-C, and paste it into a word processor of your choice.
